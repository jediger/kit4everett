My Vision for Everett

Everett Schools & Pope John Paul

Everett students are engaged, passionate, intelligent, and they deserve to receive an education that helps them live up to their potential. Overcrowding is has been impacting students for a long time, and we need to act with a sense of urgency to give them the space, attention, and choices they need to thrive. Renovating and re-opening Pope John Paul as a school in Ward 4 is a sensible way to create more space now, while additional options for another high school are explored.

Housing in Everett

Everett has a legacy of being a community where everyday working people can afford to live, raise families, and build a future. In spite of the large apartment complexes going up around the city creating more housing stock, it is becoming more and more difficult for working families to afford rent and for elders to afford increasing tax payments on their homes. Many Everett residents are facing the hard choice to leave or continue to tighten their belts to afford to stay, and we need solutions now to help the people of Everett stay in the city they love.
